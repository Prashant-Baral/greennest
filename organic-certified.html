What Does 'Organic Certified' Actually Mean?

Written by Nandini Joshi | October 5, 2024

Not all "organic" labels are created equal. As consumers become more environmentally conscious, the word "organic" is often slapped onto packaging with little regulation. However, true sustainability comes from verified, third-party certifications.

GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard)

This is the gold standard for organic textiles, such as our organic cotton bedding. It ensures the organic status of textiles from harvesting of the raw materials through environmentally and socially responsible manufacturing all the way to labeling. To achieve GOTS certification, a textile product must contain a minimum of 70% organic fibers.

OEKO-TEX® Standard 100

If a product is labeled as OEKO-TEX certified, it means that every component of the article, i.e. every thread, button, and other accessories, has been tested for harmful substances and the article therefore is harmless for human health. It does not mean it's organic, but it guarantees it's non-toxic.

Fair Trade Certified

While not strictly an environmental certification, Fair Trade ensures that the people making the products are working in safe conditions, protecting the environment, building sustainable livelihoods, and earning additional money to empower and uplift their communities.

Conclusion

When shopping for your home, look beyond the marketing buzzwords. Seek out these trusted badges on the packaging or product pages to ensure you are truly making an eco-friendly choice.
